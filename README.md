🧬 Mucin Glycobiology & Quantitative Imaging Lab

👋 About Us
We are a mucin glycobiology research lab focused on understanding how glycosylation shapes mucus barrier function, microbial ecology, and host health.
Our work integrates:
•	Quantitative in situ imaging of mucus
•	Spatial glycan mapping on mucins
•	Host–microbe interactions at mucosal surfaces
•	Non-invasive biomarker development from fecal mucus (MUC2)
 
🔬 Research Focus
🧪 1. Mucus Structure & Barrier Function
•	Organization of b1 and b2 mucus layers
•	Biophysical and biochemical properties of MUC2 networks
•	Effects of glycosylation on mucus integrity

🧬 2. Glycan Spatial Biology
•	Distribution of terminal sugars (sialic acid, fucose, sulfation)
•	Lectin-based imaging (e.g., MAL II, UEA-1)
•	Glycan-dependent structuring of mucus microdomains

🦠 3. Host–Microbiota Interactions
•	Spatial segregation of microbes from host tissues
•	Microbial colonization of mucus niches
•	Functional consequences of glycan perturbations

🧫 4. Translational Glycomics
•	Fecal MUC2 glycome profiling
•	Non-invasive biomarkers of inflammation and diet response
•	Glycan-based diagnostics and therapeutic strategies
 

🖼 Imaging & Histology
•	Confocal microscopy (3D reconstruction, z-stacks)
•	Lectin staining (MAL II, UEA-1, WGA)
•	FISH (e.g., EUB338 bacterial probe)
•	Histological staining (Alcian Blue, PAS, Carnoy fixation)
🧮 Image Analysis
•	ImageJ / Fiji (macro-based batch processing)
•	Mucus thickness quantification
•	Spatial distance measurements (e.g., bacteria–epithelium)
📊 Data Science
•	R (tidyverse, ggplot2, lme4, emmeans)
•	Mixed-effects modeling and multivariate analysis
•	Integration of imaging + glycomics + microbiome data
 

🧪 Representative Analyses
•	📏 Mucus thickness profiling
•	🧭 Spatial distribution of bacteria relative to mucus layers
•	🧬 Lectin intensity mapping across tissue regions
•	📊 Comparative glycan phenotyping across genotypes/diets

•	Diet–Microbiota–Mucus Axis
Linking glycome shifts to inflammation and microbial ecology
